## Overview

Air pollution poses significant risks to public health, environmental sustainability, and urban development. Governments and environmental agencies rely on continuous monitoring of air quality to support policy decisions and protect communities.

This project develops a synthetic urban air quality monitoring dataset that simulates cities, monitoring stations, pollutant concentrations, weather conditions, and Air Quality Index (AQI) measurements. Following the CRISP-DM (Cross-Industry Standard Process for Data Mining) methodology, the project demonstrates how synthetic environmental data can support exploratory analysis, predictive modeling, and environmental decision-making while preserving realistic relationships among variables.

## Business Problem

Environmental agencies require reliable air quality monitoring systems to identify pollution trends, issue health advisories, and support environmental planning.

However, collecting large-scale environmental datasets is expensive and often limited by sensor availability, missing observations, and inconsistent reporting across monitoring stations.

This project addresses these challenges by generating realistic synthetic environmental data capable of supporting analytics, predictive modeling, and dashboard development.

Key challenges addressed include:

- Air pollution monitoring
- Air Quality Index prediction
- Environmental trend analysis
- Weather and pollutant relationships
- Synthetic environmental data generation

---

## Dataset

This project uses a fully synthetic environmental monitoring dataset designed to simulate real-world air quality monitoring systems.

Dataset includes:

- Cities
- Monitoring stations
- Pollutant measurements (PM2.5, PM10, NO₂, O₃, CO)
- Weather conditions
- Air Quality Index (AQI)
- Population and urban characteristics
- Temporal monitoring records

The synthetic data preserves realistic environmental relationships while ensuring no real monitoring station data is exposed.
